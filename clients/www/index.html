Sputnik is a predictions market and futures trading platform.

Predictions

A prediction market allows users bet on whether a future event will come to pass.

In short:
If you believe an event will come to pass, you should buy a prediction.
If you believe an event will not come to pass, you should sell a prediction instead.

In detail:
The price of a prediction is denominated as a percentage between 0 and 100. Our site enforces a tick size of 1/10 of a percent to avoid trivial front running of orders.
A user may place an order to buy or sell at any percentage they wish. If there is an open order betting in the opposite direction already in the order book, the order will be filled at the price of open order. Otherwise, the order will be placed into the order book till it is filled or the user cancels it.
The cost of a prediction and the potential payout depends on the price the order is filled at and whether it is a buy or a sell.

Formally:
Let Y be the size of a prediction contract and X% the price of the prediction:
If it is a buy, the cost is X% of Y and the payout is (100-X)% of Y,
If it is a sell, the cost is (1-X)% of Y and the payout is X% of Y,
The cost of the prediction is deducted soon as you place an order, even if it does not immediately fill or is yet to be filled by a counter party's order. The bitcoins are returned if the order is cancelled before it is filled.
This payout scheme makes the expected value of a prediction zero if the price is accurate.

Example:

Prediction markets can be used to gauge the probability of a future event, hedge against an undesirable event, or generate profit for a savy speculator.

c.f. Robin Hanson

Futures

A future is an agreement to buy or sell an asset for a given price at a later date.

In short:
If you believe price of an asset will go up, you should buy a future.
If you believe the price of an asset will go down, you should sell a future

In detail:
The price of a future is denominated in bitcoins. A tick size, dependent on the asset, will be enforced to avoid trivial front running of orders.
A user may place an order to buy or sell at any percentage they wish. If there is an open order betting in the opposite direction already in the order book, the order will be filled at the price of open order. Otherwise, the order will be placed into the order book till it is filled or the user cancels it.
A portion of the purchase price, the margin, is required as a down payment. The margin is adjusted as the price of a future fluctuates. If a trader cannot meet the required margin, their contracts will be closed. A trader does not have to worry about their counter party's solvency, as Sputnik ensures the counter party risk.
The initial cost of a future is the margin. As the margin fluctuates, money will be taken or returned from your account to maintain the new margin.

Formally:
Let X the price of the asset, Y be the lot size of the futures contract (namely the quantity of the asset represented by a single contract), and Z the percent required for margin:
Purchasing n contracts requires at least X*Y*Z bitcoins in margin.
Margin is required as soon as you place an order, even if it does not immediately fill or is yet to be filled by a counter party's order. Margin is returned if the order is cancelled before it is filled.
